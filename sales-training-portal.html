Test Your Knowledge
15 questions across the entire sales process
Q1. What is the #1 quadrant you must absolutely know the answer to during strategic questioning?
A) Buying Criteria
B) Individual Motive
C) Risk of not working with us
D) Change readiness
Individual Motive is the emotional reason why they'll do business with us. It's the #1 priority because people buy on emotion. Buying Criteria (#2) and Risk (#3) are also critical, but motive drives the decision.
Q2. In the Staircase (CIS) process, what comes first?
A) Up the Escalator — explore the gains
B) Down the Stairs — explore the pain
C) Present the program details
D) Ask about budget
Always go Down the Stairs first (pain) — "Why is that important?", "Consequences?", "If it continues?" — then Up the Escalator (gain) — "If we helped?", "How would that feel?", "And then what?"
Q3. When presenting program breakdowns, how should you pace the delivery?
A) Read through every detail slowly and thoroughly
B) Skip the breakdown entirely
C) Move fast — people buy on emotion, not detail
D) Let the client read it themselves silently
Flick through the program breakdown fast. Do NOT read word for word — it takes too much time and people buy on emotion. Present, don't engage on the detail slides.
Q4. What's the "smoke out" question in objection handling?
A) "What's your budget?"
B) "How does that sound?"
C) "Other than that, is there anything else that would stop you from moving forward?"
D) "Would you like to pay by credit card?"
The smoke out isolates the real objection. Once they say "No, that's the only thing," you can address it confidently knowing there's nothing else hiding behind it.
Q5. How many breakthrough objectives should the client choose for themselves?
A) 1
B) 2
C) 5
D) All 10
Ask them to choose 2 for themselves AND 2 they'd like their key people to be better at. Then take each through the Down/Up staircase process.
Q6. What should you always pitch for as a minimum?
A) 1 individual enrolment
B) 3-pack
C) 6-pack (buy 5, get 1 free)
D) Inhouse only
"Always go for the cake, not the piece." Pitch 6-pack minimum. It's OK if you only get 1 or 2, but always start high. Bigger companies: offer 12-pack or inhouse.
Q7. What are the 3 details you MUST capture when closing a sale?
A) Name, company, job title
B) Name, email, smartphone number
C) Name, address, credit card
D) Name, email, LinkedIn profile
Name, email, and smartphone number are a MUST to enter the person into the system. Without these, you can't register them in the program.
Q8. In the BE / DO / GET framework, what does "BE" represent?
A) Behavioural change through emotional engagement
B) Being present in the training room
C) Being the best version of yourself
D) Before the program starts
BE = Behavioural change only happens when we're emotionally engaged. That's why we conduct awareness meetings — to help clients understand WHY it's important to improve skills.
Q9. If a client says "I just want to think about it," what's the first thing you should do?
A) Give them a week to think
B) Offer a bigger discount
C) Smoke out — "Other than that, is there anything else?"
D) Send them a follow-up email
Always smoke out first to isolate the real objection. Then respond: "That's exactly the reason why you need to move forward!" and recap their pain from the awareness discussion.
Q10. What's the investment for the 6-pack deal?
A) $18,000
B) $15,975 (buy 5, $3,000 credit on 6th)
C) $12,500
D) $20,000
The 6-pack: buy 5 enrolments at $3,195 each = $15,975, with a $3,000 credit on the 6th person. Plus a $500 discount if paid within 7 days.
Q11. When a client says their team needs to "buy in" first, what do you offer?
A) Send them brochures to share
B) Book a group presentation
C) Offer to interview their team members at no charge or obligation
D) Offer a discount to get them over the line
"We're engagement specialists — we're happy to interview your potential candidates at no charge or obligation." Then book awareness meetings with each team member and commit to a debrief within 48 hours.
Q12. What's the inhouse DCC investment?
A) $25,000+GST
B) $30,000+GST
C) $36,995+GST
D) $45,000+GST
$36,995+GST for up to 25 people, with a $995 incentive if paid within 7 days. Payment options available over 6 months.
Q13. Why does Dale Carnegie use memory techniques in their programs?
A) To make the program feel more interactive
B) Because people will only use what they can recall in real time
C) To fill time in the program schedule
D) Because the textbook is too long to reference
"Knowledge is power, but only if you can remember it." That's why Warren Buffet talks about his DC experience 50 years later — he remembers it and uses it.
Q14. If the client wants to choose another time for the follow-up meeting, what should you do?
A) Let them call you when they're ready
B) Work with them but keep it tight — within a few days
C) Book it for the following month
D) Send them a calendar link and wait
Keep follow-up meetings tight — a few days after the initial meeting. Mention that other consultants are working on the same programs to create urgency around priority placement.
Q15. What are the 3 stages that make Dale Carnegie different from other providers?
A) Plan, Execute, Review
B) Learn, Practice, Test
C) Awareness, Programs, Sustainment
D) Engage, Train, Graduate
1) Awareness — help people understand what to change and why. 2) Programs — tried and proven processes for skills and behaviours. 3) Sustainment — long-term tools to sustain the investment. "We don't love and leave our customers."

4. Interest & Collaborate — Strategic Questioning
The difference for strategic sales is your questioning and what you're listening for
▼
▶

Expert Video: Strategic Questioning

Place video file in /videos/04-questioning.mp4

"As Is" Questions — Where are they now?

Ask These
"How's business going?"
"What's working for the business?"
"What's not working?"
"Is your business performing the way you want it to?"
"If it could improve, what would the improvements be?"
"What keeps you awake at night?"
"If you could change 3 things, what would they be?"

"Should Be" Questions — Where do they want to go?

Ask These
"What should the business look like?"
"How do you see your vision?"
"Why is it important to grow?"
"Why are those changes important to the future prosperity of the company?"
"Do your people see those changes as clearly as you do?"

The Four Quadrants

#1 Individual Motive (MUST KNOW)

The emotional reason why they'll do business with us. This is the #1 quadrant you must absolutely understand.

#2 Buying Criteria (MUST KNOW)

Do they have the money? You need to know the landscape otherwise you'll waste a lot of time.

#3 Risk of NOT Working With Us

The risk of not working with us needs to be greater than any investment. Help your client see this clearly.

#4 Change

Is there anything that would stop us working together if we had the right solutions?

Org Chart Discovery

Fill out the org chart as you go. Ask about each key report: "Who looks after operations/sales/finance/customer care? What's their name? What would you like them to improve on as a leader? How would that help the business?"
Don't forget: "Obviously... you look after the company, what would you like to improve on as a leader? How would that help the business?"

Expert Video: BE DO GET

The Three Stages

BE — Behavioural change only happens when we're emotionally engaged. We conduct awareness meetings to help clients understand why it's important to improve skills.
DO — Our clients want more of the DO experience. Practical, hands-on application.
GET — When individuals shift, it affects the team. Results follow behaviour change.

9. Breakthrough Objectives & The Staircase (CIS)
Get them to choose objectives, then go Down the Stairs (pain) and Up the Escalator (gain)
▼
▶

Expert Video: The CIS Staircase Process

Place video file in /videos/09-cis-staircase.mp4

Set Up
"So you can experience the process, could you please read through the 10 breakthrough objectives and choose 2 for yourself you'd like to take to another level... I also want you to think of your senior team and tick 2 that you'd like your key people to be better at."

The Staircase Process

Why is that important?
Down 1
Consequences?
Down 2
If it continues?
PAIN
If we helped?
Up 1
How would that feel?
Up 2
And then what?
GAIN
Down the Stairs (Pain)
"You've chosen [objective]. Why is that important to you?"
"So what are the consequences of that?"
"If that was to continue, then what would happen?"
Up the Escalator (Gain)
"If we could help you [objective], what would that mean for you?"
"How would that make you feel?"
"And then what?"
Do this for 2 personal objectives, then 2 for the team. Use the same Down/Up process each time.
Confirm Commitment
"So if we could improve [2 personal objectives] as well as help your team with [2 team objectives], would you be willing to put time and effort into the process to achieve the outcomes you desire?"

Key Presentation Tips

Move FAST through program breakdowns. Do not read word for word — it takes too much time and people buy on emotion, not detail.
Breakthrough examples: "If one of your staff never said a thing in meetings, what would be a breakthrough? They talk in meetings! That's a significant shift in performance."

How We Deliver Results

Awareness: "Want to, need to, can do, show me how."
Knowledge: "We use memory techniques because people use what they can recall in real time. That's why Warren Buffet talks about his DC experience 50 years later."
Skill: "We only develop skill if we are coached effectively." Read coaching, group dynamics, and trainer from slide.

Leadership Training for Managers

3 real-time projects over 3 days: 12-month business plan, innovation/delegation to work ON the business, aligning KPIs with the plan.

12. Objection Handling
Listen, cushion, smoke out, respond with evidence
▼
▶

Expert Video: Handling Objections

Place video file in /videos/12-objections.mp4

The Objection Handling Framework

1. Listen & Cushion — Acknowledge their concern
2. Smoke Out — "Other than that, is there anything else that would stop you from moving forward?"
3. Respond — Use reverse and evidence
4. Evaluative Question — "How does that sound?"
💰 Cost
▼
Smoke Out
"I can understand that this is a fair investment. Other than that, is there anything else that would stop you from moving forward?"
Response (Self)
"That's exactly the reason why you need to move forward! You mentioned [objectives] as being very important to you [highlight pain]. That's incredibly costly over the next 12 months, let alone the next 20 years. If I could organise a payment schedule, would that help you move forward today?"
Response (Team)
"What is the real cost of non-performance over the next month? Now multiply that by 12 months — and that's not even considering the opportunity cost. We both know it's far more expensive than this investment."
⏰ Timing & Time
▼
Response (Self)
"You're worth 3 days to make those important changes we discussed! With that in mind, let's get you started."
Response (Team)
"If we could work together on a schedule of key people doing the program throughout the next twelve months, this would take the pressure off the business, and you would still achieve the upskilling your business needs."
🤝 Buy-in From Team
▼
Response
"Our job is to help you get the willing cooperation of your team. We'll talk to your nominated leaders to help them see how the process will accelerate results. If there are leaders that don't want to engage, or we don't think they're a good investment, we'll let you know at the debrief. You're still not committed until we've had that discussion. Could I speak to them now?"
Book awareness interviews within 48 hours. Commit to a debrief meeting with the decision maker — keep it tight!
⏳ Procrastination
▼
Response
"That's exactly the reason why you need to move forward! [Recap pain from awareness]. Nothing changes unless something changes — let's get you started."
😨 Fear
▼
Response
"The company has graduated 9 million people globally and your concerns are normal of that group. People choose Dale Carnegie because our methodology significantly shifts the very area that you want to improve. Nothing changes unless something changes — let's get you moving in the right direction, with a trusted vehicle."
