  • Unselfish, sensitive to others' needs, genuine
  • Long fuse, empathetic listener, builds trust naturally
  • Projects confidence without arrogance
  • High persistence on complex projects
  • Prefers long-term relationships over superficial ones

DISC-Based Focus Areas for 2026

  • Learning to say "no" to avoid spreading too thin
  • More direction toward work tasks, less chatting/socialising during critical periods
  • Setting more ambitious deadlines
  • Increased urgency in decision-making
  • Detailed delegation to reduce ambiguity
  • May hesitate to correct/discipline for fear of offending — needs to lean into "good guy not just nice guy"
  • Under stress: may become non-committal, vague, or overly tolerant of non-performers
3 Coaching Journey — 2025 in Review

Victories

  • Completed triathlon with brothers (March) — huge milestone
  • Built one of the most formidable senior teams in Australian business (Drew, Nick, Linda)
  • Broke free from poor behaviours — committed to daily morning training
  • Achieved 28% growth — outpaces most companies
  • Board relationships redesigned and strengthened
  • Consistent morning journaling and Stoic reading practice established
  • Holiday scored "15 out of 10" for family connection
  • Embracing vulnerability and authentic leadership style
  • Unlocking talent across the organisation — attracting more talent as a result
  • X2U project now ahead of traditional pipeline
  • Broke into Victorian market in back-to-base services
  • Working a productive 50 hours/week focused on high-value relationships
  • Partner relationship (Jodie) strong and balanced

Challenges Worked Through

  • Board pressure — meetings felt "just about the numbers" not vision/purpose
  • Imposter syndrome around finance/board papers — feeling "uneducated"
  • Feeling stretched — having to push to "turn up as Drew" for the team
  • Balancing empathy with accountability (nice guy vs good guy)
  • Work/life balance — lowest scored area on 360
  • Weekend dynamics with Jodie — collaborative planning vs. perceived indifference
  • Process and planning skills remain the weakest area (3.84/5)
  • Communication clarity — expressing ideas concisely (3.60 on team feedback)
  • Self-medication with alcohol — shifted to celebration-only approach

Key Frameworks Introduced

  • Cognitive Reframing (red/blue-green journaling)
  • "Facilitator of Talent" identity — not needing to know everything
  • "Protector" mindset — shielding the team while leveraging their strengths
  • "Blue Slide" concept from Diary of a CEO — IOR's version still to be created
  • Nice Guy vs Good Guy distinction
  • Power of saying less in meetings
4 2026 Coaching Plan
Q1 Jan–Mar "Foundations"
  • Continue morning routine (exercise, journal, Stoic reading)
  • Build IOR's "Blue Slide" — the company's clear why/vision/purpose one-pager
  • Set 3 personal stretch goals and share with William for accountability
  • Review board communication approach — present in ROI terms they value
Q2 Apr–Jun "Process & Structure"
  • Address weakest 360 area: Process Skills (planning, delegation, time management)
  • Implement weekly 30-min planning block — priorities for the week
  • Delegate with detailed scope: what, why, by when, then "I'll leave it in your capable hands"
  • Practice "evidence-based" communication — supporting ideas with data
Q3 Jul–Sep "Positive Discomfort"
  • Seek moments of discomfort that drive growth
  • Practice having harder accountability conversations (good guy, not just nice guy)
  • Address presentation skills — prepare structured board presentations
  • Push decision-making speed — set daily goals that test focus
Q4 Oct–Dec "Legacy & Scale"
  • Build second-tier leadership capability — who leads when Drew isn't in the room?
  • Define clear roles and responsibilities (lowest 360 item: 4.00)
  • Create Drew's personal leadership "playbook" — codify what works
  • Year-end reflection and 360 re-assessment
